🧠 What is Sign Language?

Sign language is a complete and natural visual language that uses hand gestures, facial expressions, and body movement to convey meaning. It is not a simplified form of spoken language — it has its own grammar, sentence structure, and rich cultural background.

Each country or region may have its own unique sign language. For example: ASL (American Sign Language), BSL (British Sign Language), and TİD (Turkish Sign Language). These are not mutually understandable — just like English and Turkish are different.

📜 A Colorful Journey Through History

The first known structured sign language appeared in 16th-century Spain where monks developed silent communication. In 1755, Charles-Michel de l’Épée established the first public school for the deaf in Paris. In the 1800s, sign language spread across Europe and America, becoming an educational tool.

In Turkey, Turkish Sign Language (TİD) has been used since the Ottoman Empire. Although formal education in TİD was neglected for years, today it is gaining increasing recognition and support thanks to inclusive education initiatives.

🌟 Why Learn Sign Language?

  • 💬 It allows you to communicate with deaf or hard-of-hearing individuals
  • 🧠 Enhances your brain’s ability to multitask, memorize, and focus visually
  • 🎓 Boosts career options in education, health, accessibility and public service
  • ❤️ Promotes empathy and emotional intelligence by helping bridge worlds
  • 🧩 It's fun, expressive, and opens your mind to a visual way of thinking!

📊 Did You Know?

  • 🌍 More than 70 million people use sign language globally
  • 🗺️ Over 300 different sign languages exist
  • In Turkey, 3+ million citizens have hearing impairments
  • 🏛️ Sign language is officially recognized in 60+ countries
  • 📺 In 2023, sign language interpreters appeared on 95% of live broadcasts in Europe

🏥 Where is Sign Language Used?

Sign language plays a vital role in many areas of daily life and public service:

  • 🏫 Education: Inclusive classrooms, deaf institutions, teacher training
  • 🏥 Healthcare: Doctor-patient communication, hospitals, therapy
  • ⚖️ Law & Courts: Legal interpreting for testimony and rights
  • 🎭 Art & Entertainment: Theater, music videos, performance interpreting
  • 💻 Technology: Apps, AI, VR learning environments
